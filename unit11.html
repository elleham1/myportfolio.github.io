UNIT 11 Computer Networks

In our forthcoming lesson, we will dig into the complex world of computer networks, investigating its different characteristics and applications. Computer networks are essentially systems that allow interconnected computers to share resources and communicate with one another. This topic is critical for understanding how digital information is transmitted between devices and places, enabling everything from basic internet browsing to complicated cloud computing. We'll start by defining what a computer network is, including its key components like servers, clients, and communication channels. Our lessons will provide a detailed review, preparing you for the first of three exercises designed to test your understanding of these fundamental ideas.

A computer network is a system that enables communication and resource sharing among numerous computing devices. This interconnection of computers and other devices allows users to exchange data, share resources, and cooperate more efficiently and effectively than using isolated machines. The key components of a computer network are vital to ensure its functionality and performance.

The server is a basic component of a computer network. Servers are powerful computers that provide resources, services, and data to other computers on the network, known as clients. These resources may include files, apps, and Internet access. Servers handle client requests, such as accessing a webpage or a shared software application, and provide the data or services required to fulfill those requests.
